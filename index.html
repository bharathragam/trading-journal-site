What to journal (Every Time)

  • Why I took (or skipped) the trade
  • Which rule allowed or blocked it
  • Emotion before decision
  • Emotion after decision
  • What I will do diffently next time
    •

    Trading Rules (V1)

    • No stop-loss or target → No trade
    • If emotions are high (fear, greed, stress) → No trade
    • Maximum 1 trade per day
    • If chart direction is unclear → No trade
    • Every trade must be journaled

    Not trading is also a valid decision.

    Before you trade (mandatory checklist)

    • Do I have clear stop-loss and target?
    • Is this trade based on rules ,not hope?
    • Am I calm right now?
    • Have I accepted the loss before entering?

    If any answer is NO-Do not trade.

    Our Method

    Capital Protection First (CPF) is a discipline-first approach for beginner traders with small capital.

    We follow a simple framework: Think → Decide → Trade.

    • Capital protection before profits
    • Clear decisions before taking trades
    • Discipline built through journaling and no-trade decisions

    Who This Is For (and Who It Is NOT)

    This is for:

    • Beginners or students starting with small capital (₹100–₹1,000)
    • People who overtrade, panic, or revenge trade
    • Traders who want discipline before profits

    This is NOT for:

    • Get-rich-quick seekers
    • Signal or indicator hunters
    • People who don’t want to journal or follow rules

    This project is built by a learner, for learners — evolving in public with honesty.

    Market Learning

    Market Basics

    Markets move because of demand and supply. Price changes when buyers or sellers become stronger.

    Risk Management

    Risk management means protecting your capital first. Never risk money you cannot afford to lose.

    Trading Psychology

    Emotions like fear and greed affect trading decisions. Discipline is more important than predictions.
